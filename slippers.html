Mood Slippers

For this final project we were tasked to create a wearable that portrayed the idea of Hygge and embodied everything we had learned from the course. I found that the ideation for this project was the most difficult from everything else we have done. My understanding was that electronics/tech was incompatible with Hygge so creating ideas was tough. In order to really start ideation, Hygge really needed to be understood.

So what is Hygge? Hygge cannot be easily defined. Definition range from “the art of creating intimacy,” to “coziness of the soul.” Most Danish people will agree that Hygge is simply something you know when you feel it; it’s togetherness, relaxation, coziness and comfort. To me, Hygge seems to be a state of being, whether it be with self, others or environment. Hygge is not stuff, it's momentary peace created by your environment, other people or doing what you love.

There were three very intentional things that I wanted to implement in this project; wireless communication, minimal embodiment and environment manipulation. So I ended up making Mood Slippers. When you slip the slippers on it triggers events in your house to create a cozy environment for you. The idea is that after a long day you come home and slip into something comfortable and the environment does too. The Mood Slippers in particular pour you a glass of wine, turn on some mood lighting (lamp) and plays lofi hip-hop on your Chromecast. This gets you ready to relax and unwind with a nice book or for you to simply enjoy the music.

Inspiration

My inspiration is smart homes. Like I stated before, Hygge with electronics doesn't mix well in my head so it acting as a simple controller opened up a world of possibilities when it came to home automation. During this time of isolation, taking advantage of being at home and making the project for the space was great too and opens up the potential to continue home automation in the future. There was no specific project that inspired this idea, more so the idea of home automation in general.

Final Idea

As stated before there were three very intentional things that I wanted to implement in this project; wireless communication, minimal embodiment and environment manipulation. If I was forced to create a wearable that had a cozy aesthetic the technology should be very discreet in the embodiment. There were ideas of creating a blanket the turned into a fort with mechanical arms or socks that warmed and massaged your foot but to me there was too much "noise" that it would distract (me) the user when wearing it taking away from the idea of Hygge. This also plays with the idea of wireless communication. If the wearable was the thing to do stuff then the embodiment would be super complex or limited to simple things like lights, sounds and small moving parts. I wanted the wearable to act as a remote that triggered events in your house essentially turning your house into a smart house but without the government/big tech surveillance. Mood Slippers was my solution to this. When you slip the slippers on it triggers events in your house to create a cozy environment for you. The idea is that after a long day you come home and slip into something comfortable and the environment does too. The Mood Slippers in particular pour you a glass of wine, turn on some mood lighting (lamp) and plays lofi hip-hop on your Chromecast. This gets you ready to relax and unwind with a nice book or for you to simply enjoy the music.

The project is broken down into to sides, the transmitter end - the slippers - and, the receiver end - the smart plug, pump and Python script. The slippers are very simple, they are powered by a simple 9V battery to allow for portability and houses a simple button that transmits a signal to a shared serial port. The receiver end is a bit more complicated. Attached to the receiver's breadboard is a smart plug. This is a reassembled power outlet that house a relay that is set to NO (normally on). You can control it to enable power to whatever is plugged in. This has more uses than just this project and will be utilized at home for personal use. Then we have a pump that pours you a glass of wine. This is rewired into a relay that is also set to NO. We can specify the delay before turning the pump off (LOW) to pour the perfect amount of wine. Lastly is the Python script that casts a Youtube video to your Chromecast. I used two python libraries that allowed for casting and serial communication. I set the serial port to the same one as the receiver and transmitter since you can only have one serial port open at a time without the use of eternal libraries. A python script runs in the background and waits for the signal. Once the signal is received (the same one for everthing else) the Youtube Controller casts the specified link to the Chromecast.
