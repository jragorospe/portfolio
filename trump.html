Trump Button

Tasked to create a button to represent a persona of choice, we built one for the President of the United States, Donald Trump. This button has multiple interactions which represent famous examples of his person and was built purely through JavaScript, HTML and CSS.

Github Repository

Context

For our first project of CPSC 581 we were tasked to design a button to depict a person of our choosing. After mulling it over within our group we decided to pick someone who was instantly recognizable and currently plastered all over the news; The Donald. The creative freedom and open-endedness of the project made it hard to pinpoint where we wanted to go with this but we decided on two very core principles from the start; (1) create a simple looking button with many interactions/outputs and (2) make it fun to use.

Rough Sketches

For my rough sketches I played around the idea of what a button actually was. I knew a button was different than an interactive scene or a mini game or a moving object. I played around various definitions and landed on a few main components: (1) It had to stay in place, (2) It had to respond to various inputs and, (3) It was clear that it was undoubtedly a button. We struggled on what to do since we wanted to make sure it blew the socks off of anyone who played with it and we agreed that the factor that would do so was the various interactions you can have with the button. We would rather have a seemingly boring button that did amazing things when interacted with than a game or scene that was ultimately lackluster to play with,

Refined Sketches

Resources and ideation for Donald Trump was not too difficult to find. Many, many sketches, memes, tweets and the everyday life of Trump made for a fun and interesting design session. Narrowing what we wanted to do was the more difficult part as we wanted to ensure, not only his recognizability to others but the recognizability of the interactions and outputs we would provide when using the button. We also received feedback from the class which did come in handy and helped in our final implementation.

We wanted to make sure we used our own resources to make our button more homemade so we made all the sketches and gifs from scratch. My contribution to the project was mostly on designing and sketching; responsible for all Donald Trump button design, alignment of pictures, gif creation/animation, editing and refinement of Cheeto Trump and advice on functionality and interaction design.

Interactions

There are four different ways to interact with Trump.
Idle: Trump is bored and blinks as he waits for you to interact with him. If you don't interact with him for a while he tranforms to Cheeto Trump.
Hover: Trump is happy that you are interacting with him.
Left-Click: Trump is angry that you click on him and tweets about it. If you click 6+ times Trump defends his love of China.
Right-Click: Trump builds the wall to his favourite music.
